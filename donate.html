make a difference

Smile of Hope Children's Outreach charity shop urgently need your donations of unwanted clothes, shoes and more!

The style, era and fit of your donations doesn’t matter to us at all. However, please donate clothes which are in good enough condition to be resold to somebody else. Thank you!

What can I donate?

  • Clothes (Women, men’s and children’s. All styles wanted including designer, high street and/or vintage)
  • Shoes (Paired)
  • Accessories (Bags, scarves, jewellery, hats etc)
  • Linen (e.g. Curtains, bed sheets all sizes)
  • Books & music
  • Homeware (Of all kinds)

    • No duvets or pillows.
    No damaged clothes or shoes. For example, we cannot reuse items which are very worn or have big holes or stains.

    What condition should donations be in?

    SHCO needs donations which are in good enough condition to resell, we don’t mind about the fit, style or era. When you are bagging up your donations, just ask yourself if items are in good enough condition to be resold to someone else. If the answer is yes, we definitely want them. If you have some items which are a little worn, that’s fine.

    Why donate?

    From production to disposal, our clothes have significant and harmful environmental and social costs. In the US, over 18 million tonnes of textiles are sent to landfill or incinerated every year resulting in increased waste and carbon emissions. For SHCO, the problem of clothes waste goes beyond US landfill into the fields, factories, homes and mills where our clothes are made. Embedded in every piece of wasted clothing are huge quantities of scarce natural resources like water, oil and land. At the same time, garment manufacturing takes place in very poor countries where labour is cheap and unregulated, and human rights abuses are commonplace.

    Every aspect of SHCO work aims to minimise, improve and transform the impacts of clothes – from stopping clothes being thrown away, to extending the life of clothes in our charity shop, to delivering sustainable education, to the international development projects we fund to improve environmental and working practices in the fashion industry.

    Combating clothing poverty
    in the us and haiti

    "Clear out your closet"
    campaign

    In the US and Haiti, thousands of people have very little or nothing to wear; yet, clothing poverty goes undocumented and unreported. All the while, devastating impact is felt by children and adults, alike. In many cases, students are avoiding school for fear of being bullied over their worn-out clothes. Those who do attend, could be seen with holes in their clothes and shoes that are held together with tape. The repeated absenteeism and feelings of shame have a crippling effect on children’s capacity to learn and grow. The detrimental impact of clothing poverty on self-esteem is felt equally by adults who find themselves unable to access training or suitable employment opportunities. Beyond the detrimental mental health implications that clothing poverty has, it also affects physical wellbeing, whereby many families who are unable to afford adequate clothing face a higher risk of falling ill.

    The solution, however, could be simple.  If clothes were redirected for good use, clothing poverty could be alleviated at very little or almost no cost.

    At SHCO, we wanted to do our part in combatting clothing poverty. To that end, we launched ‘Clear out your Closet’ campaigns to encourage our teams in South Florida to declutter their wardrobes over the period of a month and donate to those who are most in need. Our 30 days of decluttering initiative began and through our clothing donation drive we filled a couple of vans and sent them on their way, where we trust our donations will make a difference to those who have nothing to wear.
